What is DDMRP
Demand Driven Material Requirements Planning — critical evolution of classic MRP (Ptak & Smith, 2011)

Problem: bullwhip effect

In classic MRP, a small demand fluctuation amplifies upstream through the BOM, causing overstock, stockouts, and very long perceived lead times.

DDMRP solution

Decoupling points are deliberately inserted along the BOM. Upstream: push logic. Downstream: pull logic. Uncertainty is absorbed locally by buffers.

5 operative components

① Strategic buffer positioning  ② Buffer zone sizing  ③ Dynamic adjustments  ④ Demand-driven planning  ⑤ Visible & collaborative execution

Key advantage

The customer's perceived lead time shrinks: when an order arrives, the lead time to cover runs only from the nearest buffer to the finished product.

Two-phase algorithm
How decoupling points are selected — Customer Tolerance Time (CTT) = 12 days
Phase 1 — Protection
Selects purchased items (source=buy) whose cumulative LT exceeds the CTT, or with high supply variability. → Result: MP1, C1, C3 buffered. Residual DLT of FP = 14 d (still > 12).
Phase 2 — Compression
If DLT > CTT, iteratively finds the best candidate (max DLT reduction × strategic score). → Adds SA1: DLT drops to 7 d < 12. Stop.

Buffer zones: Red / Yellow / Green
Each decoupling point has three operative zones calculated on ADU and individual DLT
Green (TOG) min order = max(ADU × DLT × LTF, MOQ) — baseline replenishment quantity
Yellow (TOY) order point = ADU × DLT — net position falls here → generate replenishment order
Red (TOR) alert = ADU × DLT × LTF × (1 + VF) — below this threshold: top priority

Lead Time Factor (LTF)

0.7 if DLT < 8 d  |  0.5 if 8–20 d  |  0.3 if > 20 d. Paradox: longer LT → smaller safety fraction, because the yellow zone is already large.

Variability Factor (VF)

Additional margin proportional to combined demand and supply uncertainty. Higher variability → higher red zone → more safety stock.

Operational summary
DDMRP turns strategic positioning into daily operational logic
1
Identify critical BOM nodes: long LT, high variability, shared components (commonality)
2
Position buffers at those nodes — phase 1 protection, phase 2 DLT compression below CTT
3
Size Red/Yellow/Green zones using ADU × DLT with variability and LTF adjustments
4
Manage daily: net position below TOY → generate order; below TOR → red alert, top priority
5
Result: customer perceived LT = 7 d vs 33 d without buffers — using only 4 active buffers on 6 items
